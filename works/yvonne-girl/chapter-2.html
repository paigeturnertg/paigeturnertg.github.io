Yvonne Girl

Chapter Two

Evaning The Score

The medical facility didn't look like the kind of place that would reshape American politics. Tucked into a nondescript office park on the outskirts of Arlington, it could have been a dermatology clinic or a boutique plastic surgery center. Which, Yvonne supposed, wasn't entirely inaccurate—it was plastic surgery, just taken to an unprecedented extreme.

Dr. Sarah Winters met them in a consultation room that managed to be both sterile and welcoming, decorated with abstract art that probably cost more than Yvonne's law school tuition. The doctor herself was in her late forties, with the kind of polished appearance that suggested she might be a satisfied customer of her own technology.

"The process is more sophisticated than traditional cosmetic procedures," Dr. Winters explained, pulling up holographic displays that showed rotating 3D models of human anatomy. "We're not just changing surface features—we're implementing comprehensive biological restructuring at the cellular level."

Yvonne studied the displays with a lawyer's attention to detail, looking for the catch, the legal liability, the thing that would make this all fall apart under scrutiny. "And the polling data supports this approach?"

David nodded, sliding a tablet across the table. "We've run extensive focus groups. The demographics we're losing—suburban women, young professionals, moderate voters—they all respond significantly better to candidates who reflect their experiences and backgrounds. The gender gap in our polling is killing us."

"So the solution is to... transform the candidate?" Yvonne couldn't quite keep the skepticism out of her voice. "Won't voters notice when the candidate suddenly looks completely different?"

"That's where you come in," Dr. Winters said, her smile containing layers of meaning that Yvonne was just beginning to unpack. "We're not proposing transforming the candidate. We're proposing transforming the campaign's most valuable asset—its legal strategist. Someone who already knows every policy position, every strategic advantage, every argument that needs to be made."

The room seemed to tilt slightly as Yvonne processed the implications. They weren't asking her to help implement this plan. They were asking her to become it.
