Yvonne Girl

Chapter Three

Cross, Dressing

The transformation process began with wardrobe consultations, which seemed almost absurdly mundane given the magnitude of the biological changes to come. Yet as stylist Marcus Rivera explained, the external presentation was just as crucial as the physical transformation itself.

"We're not just creating a candidate," Marcus said, holding up fabric swatches against Yvonne's current complexion. "We're creating a narrative. Every outfit, every accessory, every choice sends a message to voters about who you are and what you represent."

Yvonne found herself standing in front of three-way mirrors, examining designer suits in cuts and styles she'd never considered wearing. The clothing was professional yet approachable, powerful yet relatable—the carefully calibrated uniform of a female candidate navigating the impossible tightrope of modern politics.

"Try to think of it as costume design for the role of a lifetime," Marcus suggested, but Yvonne knew it was more than that. This wasn't cosplay or method acting. This was rewriting her fundamental identity in service of electoral mathematics.

As she examined herself in the mirror, holding up a navy blue blazer with subtle pinstripes, Yvonne wondered which parts of herself she'd recognize when all of this was over. Would she still see the ambitious law school graduate who'd fought for every opportunity? Or would she see someone else entirely—someone created by polling data and campaign strategy?

The answer, she suspected, was somewhere in between. And that uncertainty was almost as transformative as the medical procedure waiting for her tomorrow.
